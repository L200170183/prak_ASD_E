<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Knowledge Economic Index</h1>

<p>The Knowledge Indexes were designed as an interactive tool for benchmarking a country's position vis-a-vis others in the global <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_economy" title="Knowledge economy">knowledge economy</a>. It was created by the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Bank_Institute" title="World Bank Institute">World Bank Institute</a> using the Knowledge Assessment Methodology (KAM).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Knowledge_Index">Knowledge Index</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Knowledge_Economic_Index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Knowledge Index">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>Knowledge Index</b> or KI is an economic indicator prepared by the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Bank_Institute" title="World Bank Institute">World Bank Institute</a>
to measure a country's ability to generate, adopt and diffuse
knowledge. Methodologically, the KI is the simple average of the
normalized performance scores of a country or region on the key
variables in three Knowledge Economy pillars - education and human
resources, the innovation system and information and communication
technology (ICT).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Knowledge_Economic_Index">Knowledge Economic Index</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Knowledge_Economic_Index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Knowledge Economic Index">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <b>Knowledge Economy Index</b> (KEI) takes into account whether the environment is conducive for knowledge to be used effectively for <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_development" title="Economic development">economic development</a>.
It is an aggregate index that represents the overall level of
development of a country or region towards the Knowledge Economy. The
KEI is calculated based on the average of the normalized performance
scores of a country or region on all 4 pillars related to the knowledge
economy - economic incentive and institutional regime, education and
human resources, the innovation system and ICT.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_4_pillars_of_the_Knowledge_Economy_framework">The 4 pillars of the Knowledge Economy framework</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Knowledge_Economic_Index&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The 4 pillars of the Knowledge Economy framework">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>An <b>economic and institutional regime</b> to provide incentives for the efficient use of existing and new knowledge and the flourishing of entrepreneurship;</li>
<li>An <b>educated and skilled population</b> to create, share, and use knowledge well;</li>
<li>An efficient <b>innovation system</b> of firms, research centers,
universities, consultants and other organizations to tap into the
growing stock of global knowledge, assimilate and adapt it to local
needs, and create new technology;</li>
<li><b>Information and communication technology</b> to facilitate the effective creation, dissemination, and processing of information.</li></ul>
